Disability Confident Portal

Four separate journeys, not one linear path. Each opens independently and can be used on its own — nothing here requires you to complete another one first.

1. Recruitment →

Check a job advert or interview plan before you hire.

Used by recruiters and hiring managers

2. Onboarding →

Support a new starter from day one, whoever hired them.

Used by line managers and HR

3. Adjustments →

Check a workplace barrier and get advice on fair support.

Used by line managers and HR, any time

4. Employee support →

Guidance for jobseekers and employees themselves.

Used by candidates and employees directly

Why these are separate

A person can be onboarded, or need an adjustment, without their advert or interview ever having been checked here. Forcing one linear journey would break for anyone hired outside this tool, or any adjustment raised outside recruitment entirely.

Ask about work and benefits

I've just been offered a job. Do I need to tell them about my condition, and will it affect my PIP?
You don't have to disclose a condition unless it's relevant to a specific adjustment you need — it's your choice. PIP is not affected by starting work or how much you earn, since it's based on the impact of your condition, not your income.
Source: GOV.UK — PIP eligibility; Scope employment toolkit

Try: "how do I ask for adjustments", "will work affect my benefits", "access to work", "do I have to tell my employer". Each answer is matched from a small knowledge base and shows its source — anything unmatched gets an honest "I don't have guidance on that" reply rather than a guess.
